Take control of your delivery network

Last-mile logistics is a challenging business with multiple moving parts. Your customers deserve a great delivery experience and you deserve a better way to manage the complexity.

Our vision is to give you one streamlined system that helps you to coordinate and control the multiple local carriers across large geographies so your customers have a unified and reliable service.

Let’s take a look at how Zoopit’s advanced software can help you to avoid mishaps and waste in last-mile logistics.

Why can’t we control our last-mile logistics?

This is the big question for logistics leaders in every country. Using multiple 3PLs, or a mix of own-fleet plus 3PL providers can be extremely difficult to manage while providing a unified delivery experience for your customers. Spoiler: your customers don’t care who is doing the delivery, they just want their goods quickly and reliably.

1. You lack oversight on deliveries once they are dispatched

When your customers are ordering from you, you have full control of the experience, right? You can change things on your website, you can brief your sales people or support team with new policies. However, once you hand over the order goods for dispatch by your 3PL you lose all that control.

Your customers don’t distinguish between you and your transporter though. In their minds if the delivery experience is poor in any way, then that is going to damage your brand. In fact, PWC research shows just one bad experience with a brand will result in 32% of those customers not ordering from you again! Negative reviews on your delivery providers will mention your brand, not the transporters in many cases.

Added to this, if you are using multiple 3PLs, you will not have a single overview for reporting on activity or KPIs. Each transporter will have different reporting tools, terminology, statistics and insight. The levels of detail and accuracy given to you, as their customer, can also vary widely.

All of this makes managing deliveries complex and challenging.

2. You can’t communicate with your customers about the delivery

It’s fair to say that transport companies and 3PLs are not known for their superb communication abilities to end-recipients. Most transporters will typically send the recipient an email when their goods are dispatched and after that it is anyone’s guess whether that delivery arrives before, during or after the time window they said.

Effective communication at this stage of the ordering process is absolutely critical if you are to retain customers and grow your business. Your customers will, in many cases, need to know exactly when a delivery is coming, especially when they need to personally accept the delivery, or prepare for the arrival.

Good communication involves accurate time windows, real-time delivery updates and open lines of contact between the recipient and the delivery team. If this is not in place for you, then your delivery system is not only letting your customers down, it is letting you down too.

3. You can’t offer support to your customers when deliveries go wrong

Your customer calls you asking:

  1. Where is my delivery?
  2. Why is my delivery late?
  3. Why did I get the incorrect package?

If you don’t have immediate access to a system that can tell you the answer to that question then you will have serious issues retaining that customer in the future.

If you have to go to your 3PL provider to follow up on a failed or delayed delivery you can often be waiting days for an answer. This puts you in an impossible position with your customer who sees you as the problem.

Of course, with good communication processes in place you don’t have these issues in the first place as customers have been kept informed of their delivery status and have checked the goods when they arrived so proof of successful delivery is captured by the delivery team.

4. Your customers don’t get a consistent delivery experience

A consistent delivery experience from your company means, regardless of region, 3PL provider, or delivery type, your customer gets the same experience every time. The same dispatch emails, the same SMS notifications, the same follow-up emails, and so on.

The delivery experience is, in many cases, the only physical interaction your customer has with you and your brand. Having this experience vary from delivery to delivery, from region to region, is not good enough for your customers and is damaging for your brand.
